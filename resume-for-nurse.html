What to include

A targeted professional summary

Three to four lines that position you for the role. Mention your licence type, years of experience, specialisation, and one quantified achievement. "ICU registered nurse with 7 years of experience managing critically ill patients, specialising in sepsis care and mechanical ventilation" is far stronger than "compassionate nurse".

Clinical experience with quantified outcomes

Each role should include facility name, unit type, dates, patient population, and three to five achievement-focused bullets. Quantify outcomes: medication accuracy rates, patient satisfaction scores, mentoring outcomes, quality improvement initiatives.

Licences and credentials

RN, LPN, LVN, or advanced practice licence (NP, CNS, CRNA, CNM) with the issuing state or country and validity dates. Hospital ATS systems often filter by licence type and state — list yours clearly and match the job requirements. Include licence numbers if relevant.

Certifications

Clinical skills and specialisations

Group your clinical skills by category: patient care, medication administration, IV therapy, wound care, ventilator management, EHR systems (Epic, Cerner, Meditech). Mirror the terminology of the job description so both recruiters and ATS systems recognise the match.

Quality improvement and leadership

Committee participation, quality improvement initiatives, preceptor roles, charge nurse experience. These demonstrate leadership and are valued for senior nursing roles. Quantify outcomes — "reduced fall rate by 35% through implementation of hourly rounding protocol".

Tips for a stronger resume

Quantify your clinical impact

Every bullet should have a metric. "Maintained 100% medication administration accuracy over 3 years", "reduced fall rate by 35% through hourly rounding", "improved patient satisfaction scores by 22% over 18 months". Numbers make clinical outcomes concrete and comparable across candidates.

List licences and certifications prominently

RN, LPN, LVN, BLS, ACLS, and specialty certifications are critical differentiators. List them in dedicated sections near the top of your document. Mention validity dates and renewal status. Hospital ATS systems often filter by licence type and state — list yours clearly.

Be specific about clinical settings

Include facility name, unit type (ICU, ER, Med-Surg, L&D, etc.), patient population, and skill mix. Mirror the exact terminology of the job description so ATS systems recognise the match. "Med-Surg" and "ICU" are very different — be precise.

Switch on ATS Mode for hospital portals

Workday, Taleo, UKG, and healthcare-specific platforms are widely used in hospitals. The single-column ATS layout parses reliably across all of these. Save the styled version for direct recruiter emails or networking events.

Highlight EHR system experience

Experience with Epic, Cerner, Meditech, or Allscripts is increasingly valued. List these in your skills section. Mention specific examples of how you have used EHR systems to improve documentation efficiency or patient outcomes.

Mention quality improvement initiatives

Committee participation, quality improvement projects, and protocol development demonstrate leadership. Quantify outcomes — "reduced fall rate by 35% through implementation of hourly rounding protocol", "reduced central line infections by 50% through bundle compliance initiative".

Include preceptor and charge nurse experience

If you have mentored new graduates or served as charge nurse, mention it explicitly with measurable outcomes. These demonstrate leadership and are valued for senior nursing roles. "Mentored 8 new graduate nurses through preceptorship programme, with 100% retention rate at 12 months".

Common mistakes to avoid

Listing responsibilities instead of outcomes

"Provided patient care" tells the recruiter nothing. "Maintained 100% medication administration accuracy over 3 years across 200+ patients" tells them exactly what you achieved. Quantify clinical outcomes wherever possible.

Omitting licence and certification details

RN, LPN, LVN, BLS, ACLS, and specialty certifications are critical. Hospital ATS systems often filter by licence type and state — omitting these details can get your application rejected automatically. List them in dedicated sections with validity dates.

Vague unit or patient population descriptions

"Worked in hospital" tells the recruiter nothing. "Staff RN in 24-bed medical ICU at tertiary academic medical center, caring for critically ill patients with sepsis, ARDS, and post-cardiac arrest conditions" tells them exactly what your experience is. Be specific.

Going over two pages

Nursing resumes should be one to two pages. One page for new graduate nurses; two pages for experienced nurses with multiple roles, certifications, and quality improvement initiatives to detail. Three pages is too many for most nursing roles.

Including irrelevant personal details

Date of birth, marital status, religion, and national ID numbers have no place on a modern nursing resume. They expose employers to discrimination claims and add nothing to your application. Stick to contact details and professional information.
