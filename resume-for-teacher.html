Teaching resumes vary significantly by country and school type. Public school districts in the US, UK, Canada, and Australia typically require specific teaching certifications, background checks, and structured application processes. Private schools, international schools, and tutoring roles have more flexible conventions but still value clear evidence of teaching capability.

Certifications are critical for teachers. List your teaching licence (state, province, or country), subject endorsements, and any additional certifications (Special Education, ESL, Montessori, IB, etc.) with the issuing body and validity dates. School district ATS systems often filter by certification — make sure yours are listed clearly and match the job requirements.

Classroom experience should be described with the same rigour as any other work experience. Include the school, grade level(s) taught, subject(s), dates, and three to five achievement-focused bullets. Quantify outcomes where possible — "improved average student test scores by 18% over two years", "mentored 3 student teachers who went on to secure teaching positions", "developed curriculum adopted across 4 schools in the district".

Teaching resumes are typically one to two pages. One page is acceptable for early-career teachers; two pages is standard for experienced teachers with multiple roles, certifications, and professional development to detail. Three pages is acceptable for senior roles (department head, principal) with extensive history.

What to include

A targeted professional summary

Three to four lines that position you for the role. Mention your teaching certification, years of experience, subject area, and one quantified achievement. "Certified secondary mathematics teacher with 8 years of experience improving student outcomes in diverse urban classrooms" is far stronger than "passionate educator".

Teaching experience with quantified outcomes

Each role should include school name, grade level(s) taught, subject(s), dates, and three to five achievement-focused bullets. Quantify outcomes: improved test scores, student progression rates, mentoring outcomes, curriculum development, extracurricular leadership.

Education and teaching credentials

Degree, institution, and graduation year. For teachers, professional teaching certification (state, province, or country) is critical. List this in a Certifications section with the issuing body and validity dates. Include any additional endorsements (Special Education, ESL, Gifted Education).

Certifications and professional development

Teaching licence, subject endorsements, and additional certifications (Special Education, ESL, Montessori, IB, Google Certified Educator, Apple Teacher, etc.). List with the issuing body and validity dates. School district ATS systems often filter by certification — list yours clearly.

Classroom and pedagogical skills

Group your skills by category: classroom management, curriculum design, assessment, differentiated instruction, technology integration (Google Classroom, Canvas, SeeSaw), special needs education. Mirror the terminology of the job description so both recruiters and ATS systems recognise the match.

Extracurricular and community involvement

Club leadership, sports coaching, drama productions, community partnerships, parent engagement initiatives. These demonstrate commitment beyond the classroom and are valued by school administrators. Mention specific outcomes — "expanded debate club from 12 to 47 members in two years".

Tips for a stronger resume

Quantify your classroom impact

Every bullet should have a metric. "Improved average student test scores by 18% over two years", "mentored 3 student teachers who secured teaching positions", "developed curriculum adopted across 4 schools in the district". Numbers make teaching outcomes concrete and comparable across candidates.

List certifications prominently

Teaching licence, subject endorsements, and additional certifications are critical differentiators. List them in a Certifications section near the top of your document. Mention validity dates and renewal status. School district ATS systems often filter by certification — list yours clearly.

Tailor for the school type

Public school districts want certification and standards alignment. Private schools want pedagogical approach and cultural fit. International schools want international experience and IB or Cambridge curriculum experience. Tailor your summary, skills, and section order for each application.

Switch on ATS Mode for school district portals

Highlight curriculum development experience

If you have developed curriculum, mention it explicitly with measurable adoption. "Developed Year 9 mathematics curriculum adopted across 4 schools in the district" is far stronger than "helped with curriculum planning". Curriculum development is a key differentiator for senior teaching roles.

Mention technology integration

Experience with Google Classroom, Canvas, SeeSaw, Kahoot, Nearpod, or other educational technology is increasingly valued. List these tools in your skills section. Mention specific examples of how you have used technology to enhance learning outcomes.

Include extracurricular contributions

Club leadership, sports coaching, drama productions, community partnerships — these demonstrate commitment beyond the classroom. Mention specific outcomes. School administrators value teachers who contribute to the wider school community.

Common mistakes to avoid

Listing responsibilities instead of outcomes

"Taught Year 8 mathematics" tells the recruiter nothing. "Improved Year 8 mathematics proficiency by 23% over two academic years, with 92% of students meeting or exceeding grade-level standards" tells them exactly what you achieved. Quantify outcomes wherever possible.

Omitting teaching certification details

Teaching licence, subject endorsements, and validity dates are critical. School district ATS systems often filter by certification — omitting these details can get your application rejected automatically. List certifications in a dedicated section with the issuing body and validity dates.

Including irrelevant personal details

Date of birth, marital status, religion, and political affiliation have no place on a teaching resume. They expose employers to discrimination claims and add nothing to your application. Stick to contact details and professional information.

Going over two pages

Teaching resumes should be one to two pages. One page for early-career teachers; two pages for experienced teachers with multiple roles and certifications. Three pages is acceptable only for senior roles (department head, principal) with extensive history.

Using vague pedagogical language

"Student-centred approach" and "differentiated instruction" are buzzwords every teacher uses. Demonstrate these through specific examples: "designed tiered learning activities for mixed-ability Year 9 classes, resulting in 18% improvement in lower-quartile student outcomes". Show, do not just claim.
